What Data Brokers Know About You

Data brokers are companies that collect, aggregate, and sell personal information. They combine your browser fingerprint with public records, purchase history, social media activity, and location data to build detailed profiles — often without your knowledge or consent.

Your browser fingerprint alone can be used to track you across websites without cookies. Combined with the data below, brokers can infer your income, health interests, political views, and more.
